In Chapter One of "Little Women" by Louisa May Alcott, the four March sisters - Jo, Meg, Beth, and Amy - express their discontent about the lack of Christmas presents due to their family's financial struggles during the Civil War. Despite their disappointment, they reflect on the importance of family and the sacrifices they need to make for the war effort. The chapter also introduces their father's absence, who is away serving as a chaplain in the army. The girls decide to forego personal gifts and instead plan to surprise their mother with thoughtful presents. The chapter ends with a letter from their father, bringing comfort and warmth to the family. The narrative highlights the sisters' different personalities, their struggles, and the bond they share.
